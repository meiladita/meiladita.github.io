Paper Journal Indonesian Physical Review as an Author

(26 December 2022)

My first paper journal at the Indonesian Physical Review Volume 6 Issue 1, January 2023 as an Author, with a paper title: "First-principles Study of Magnetic Anisotropy Energy in Two-dimensional Ferromagnetic CrI3" and published on December 26, 2022. This paper very comperehensive and detailed to discuss the theoritical approach for magnetic anisotropy energy (MAE) in 2D ferromagnetic CrI3. Here we include the MAE part from the magnetocrystalline anisotropy energy (MCAE) that comes from spin-orbit coupling (SOC). In the present calculation, the MCAE part, in addition to a common scheme of total energy (TE), was evaluated using a grand-canonical force theorem scheme.

Final Project Bachelor of Physics

(August 2021 - February 2022)

My research is about magnetic anisotropy in two-dimensional (2D) materials magnetic for sensors and spintronics device design with spin density functional theory (SDFT). The calculation was implemented in house code using computer parallel (cluster computer) of 4 nodes with 20 cores each from the Division of Mathematical and Physical Science, Kanazawa University, Japan. We calculated magnetic anisotropy energy (MAE) in 2D monolayer CrI3 ferromagnetic material, contributing from the magnetocrystalline anisotropy energy (MCAE). The MCAE contribution is calculated using two methods: total energy calculation and the grand-canonical force theorem (GCFT). Then from the GCFT, we calculated atomic-resolved MCAE, k-resolved MCAE, and atomic k-resolved MCAE. We make an effort to explain the origin of perpendicular MCAE. I have set myself to concentrate on studying computational material science.
