Final Project Bachelor of Physics

(August 2017 - February 2022)

My research is about magnetic anisotropy in a two-dimensional (2D) materials for spintronics application with density functional theory (DFT). The calculation was implemented in house code using computer parallel (cluster computer) of 4 nodes with 20 cores each from the Division of Mathematical and Physical Science, Kanazawa University, Japan. We calculated magnetic anisotropy energy (MAE) in 2D monolayer CrI3 ferromagnetic material which contribution from the magnetocrystalline anisotropy energy (MCAE). The MCAE contribution is calculated using the two methods, namely total energy calculation and the grand-canonical force theorem (GCFT). Then from the GCFT, we calculated atomic-resolved MCAE, k-resolved MCAE, and atomic k-resolved MCAE. We make an effort to explain the origin of perpendicular MCAE. I have set myself to concentrate on studying computational science.
